Summary


The Proposal
June 19, 2009

For three years, Andrew Paxton has had a hard time as the assistant to Margaret Tate, hard-driving editor at a New York publisher. When Margaret, a Canadian, faces deportation for an expired visa, she hatches a scheme to marry Andrew - he agrees if she'll promise a promotion. A skeptical immigration agent vows to test the couple about each other the next Monday. Andrew had plans to fly home that weekend for his grandma's 90th, so Margaret goes with him - to Sitka, Alaska - where mom, dad, and grams await. Margaret and Andrew must stick to their wedding plan despite numerous mishaps.

He says this is all he can give me and I said I didn't need any of it. Then he pulled out a rose and kneed on my carpet. He said "Will you marry me." I was at a loss of words from the sudden confession, since I met him once on the cruise. I said "no" and frantically waved my hands in a way to express my emotions.
