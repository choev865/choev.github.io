Summary


Grizzly Man
August 12, 2005

Pieced together from Timothy Treadwell's actual video footage, Werner Herzog's remarkable documentary examines the calling that drove Treadwell to live among a tribe of wild grizzly bears on an Alaskan reserve. A devoted conservationist with a passion for adventure, Timothy believed he had bridged the gap between human and beast. When one of the bears he loved and protected tragically turns on him, the footage he shot serves as a window into our understanding of nature and its grim realities.


Dream
March 2014

It was a beautiful, sunny day and I was in an empty forest with my whole family. My parents, grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins, etc. were there and we all brought food for our picnic. We were chatting and enjoying the food until a bear came out of nowhere and stabbed my grandma in the eye. Everyone screamed and the bear ran back into his home, which was a huge cave. I started crying and my relatives ran after the bear with a huge sword.

Veronica

•••◦◦
Crazy
I originally watched this documentary for my class and I thought Treadwell was crazy. It was a documentary that kept you on your toes due to Treadwell's way of living. He believed he had a special relationship with the bears, so he lived in nature with them and spoke to them. I also thought the shots of the bears in the wild from the producers and Treadwell's own recordings were stunning and beautiful.
